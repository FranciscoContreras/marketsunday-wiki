If you have ever built a copper golem — a carved pumpkin set on a block of copper — you know they are charming and just a little aimless. Work Orders gives them a calling. Craft a special piece of paper, hand it to a golem, and it takes up honest work: hauling cargo between chests, stoking furnaces, tidying dropped items, sorting a storage hall, or tending your fields. The copper folk stay exactly what they are in vanilla — they oxidize, they take a name tag, they can be waxed with a honeycomb — they simply have somewhere to be now.

## The promise

- **No commands.** Everything you do happens in the world: craft, right-click, tap. There is nothing to type, ever.
- **Tap-to-route.** A golem learns its route from you tapping containers — pickup, then drop-off — while its own action-bar messages guide each step.
- **Item-safe.** Every transfer is a real move. Items are never duplicated and never voided; if a drop-off is full, the golem holds the cargo and retries until space opens.
- **Vanilla-hearted.** No gadgets, no held tools — a floating copper nameplate is the whole uniform. Name tags, leads, honeycomb, and axes all still work the way you expect.

Servers can gate individual jobs behind permissions, but all six are open by default.

## How assignment works

Craft the Work Order for the job you want and right-click a copper golem while holding it — the paper is consumed as the golem accepts the post. A thirty-second window then opens for you to tap the containers it should work between, and the golem's action-bar messages walk you through each tap. That is the whole ceremony; the step-by-step walkthrough lives in [Getting Started](/work-orders/getting-started/).

:::tip
Wondering what a golem is up to? Right-click it with an empty hand and the action bar answers with its job, status, and oxidation — "Courier │ hauling │ exposed", for instance.
:::
